## 5. Posting Schedule

90-day cadence:

- 3 posts per week
- 2 technical/project posts
- 1 learning/reflection post
- 10 meaningful comments per week on AI startup, recruiter, and ML engineer posts

Weekly pattern:

- Monday: project build log
- Wednesday: technical explanation
- Friday: learning/reflection or internship update

Monthly themes:

- Days 1-30: credibility foundation
  - polish profile
  - publish project breakdowns
  - connect with founders, recruiters, ML engineers

- Days 31-60: technical depth
  - post architecture diagrams
  - share model/API decisions
  - ask thoughtful questions under startup engineering posts

- Days 61-90: opportunity push
  - post internship availability
  - share project demos
  - message recruiters and founders with specific project relevance

## 7. How to Approach Recruiters

Recruiter message:

```text
Hi [Name], I am Kunj Patel, a CS student and AI/ML developer open to internship opportunities.

I have built:
- Lumina AI: document intelligence with OCR, semantic chunking, summarization, Firebase auth, FastAPI
- MoodLens: transformer-based emotion and sarcasm detection
- AI Fitness Coach: computer vision pushup analysis with OpenCV and MediaPipe

I am looking for AI/ML, NLP, computer vision, or applied AI internship roles where I can contribute to model workflows, APIs, and product integration.

Would it be okay if I shared my portfolio and resume?
```

Follow-up after 5-7 business days:

```text
Hi [Name], just following up on my previous note. I am still very interested in AI/ML internship opportunities and would be glad to share my portfolio if relevant. Thank you.
```

Authenticity rules:

- Share real progress, not exaggerated outcomes
- Be specific about what you built
- Mention tradeoffs and mistakes
- Avoid pretending projects are bigger than they are
- Show learning velocity and product thinking
